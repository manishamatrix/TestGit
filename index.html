Why Psilocybin?

Psychosocial stressors such as elevated cortisol levels suppress the body’s ability to naturally produce regulated levels of estrogen, progesterone and testosterone in both men and women. We are increasingly living in environmentals that create external stress Psychedelics are demonstrating promising results in the treatment of pro-inflammatory cytokines and hyperactive HPA therefore reducing the body’s production of cortisol and/or fight or flight response.

By putting the body into a parasympathetic state, we can control sleep patterns, gut issues, blood pressure, all contributing to healthy production of our sex hormones. Along with a healthy gut microbiome addressed through our dietary protocol, the psilocybin treatment is a customisable dosage and treatment cycle depending on the patient’s needs

Our Approach

The gut-brain axis is a bidirectional communication network that links the enteric and central nervous systems. This network includes the endocrine, humoral, metabolic, and immune routes of communication. The autonomic nervous system, hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis, and nerves within the gastrointestinal tract, all link the gut and the brain, allowing the brain to influence intestinal activities, including activity of functional immune effector cells; and the gut to influence mood, cognition, mental health and hormone regulation.

So what is happening when you microdose? Psilocybin has the profound effect to act on specific receptors 2A and 2C that act as a gate keeper to other monoamine neurotransmitters. This signals feel good hormone production to the gut microbiome whilst the gut produces 90% of the body’s serotonin which is absorbed through plasma. This effect regulates the immune, metabolic endocrine system, stress and sex  hormones like cortisol leading to the balanced production of our progesterone, estrogen and testosterone.
